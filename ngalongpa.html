Ngalogpa

Welcome to western Bhutan!

Intro

Dzongkhags: Chhukha, Punakha, Dagana, Wangdi Phodrang, Paro, Thimphu, Gasa, Haa.

The Ngalop people speak Dzongkha, which is the main Bhutanese language and is spoken by the government and in schools. Tibetan Buddhism is their main religion. A smaller number of them follow the Bon religion, which is similar. Though the language is the same, the accent is very different. For example, people from Punakha tend to be more polite, while people from Haa and Paro tend to be harsh.



Facts

The Ngalop eat red rice, potatoes, and barley. Their houses are built of timber, stone, bricks, and clay. Most of the government and the monarchy are Ngalop. They celebrate a new year festival called Lomba where people make different dishes and gather together with their family. People from these regions mostly prefer to eat spicy foods like the famous dish ema datshi.
